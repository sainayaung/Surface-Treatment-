Zone 01 — Pre-Treatment

Before any plating begins, the surface must be completely clean and chemically activated. Any oil, rust, or oxide will cause the coating to fail. This zone is most critical for adhesion quality.

🔩
STEP PRE-00
Receiving & Racking
Purpose
Parts received, inspected for defects, then mounted onto racks or loaded into barrels for processing through the line.
Equipment
Titanium racks, plastic/steel barrels, copper hooks, crane or hoist system
Key Points
Rack contact points are masked to prevent unplated spots. Part orientation affects current distribution.
VISUAL CHECK
BARREL OR RACK
🔥
STEP PRE-01
Degreasing / Cleaning
Purpose
Remove oils, grease, cutting fluids, fingerprints, and stamping lubricants from the metal surface.
Chemical
Alkaline cleaner (NaOH-based), 40–70°C, pH 10–13
Method
Soak or electrolytic. Electrolytic is more powerful — gas bubbles scrub the surface mechanically.
Time
5–15 min soak; 2–5 min electrolytic
HOT SOLUTION
HEATED TANK
BLOWER AGITATION
💧
STEP PRE-02
Rinse After Degreasing
Purpose
Wash off alkaline cleaner to prevent contaminating the next bath. Carry-over causes chemical imbalance.
Method
Double counter-flow rinse (2 tanks) — clean water in Tank 2 overflows into Tank 1 continuously
Water
Tap or DI water. Conductivity monitored to know when to replace.
COUNTER-FLOW
2 RINSE TANKS
⚗️
STEP PRE-03
Acid Pickling / Etching
Purpose
Remove rust, mill scale, and oxide layers. Leaves a chemically active, bare metal surface.
Chemical
HCl or H₂SO₄, 5–30%, room temp or slightly heated
Time
1–10 min depending on oxide thickness. Over-pickling causes hydrogen embrittlement.
Note
Inhibitors added to slow base metal attack while still removing oxides.
CORROSIVE ACID
EXHAUST REQUIRED
PP TANK
⚙️
STEP PRE-04
Activation / Pre-dip
Purpose
Final chemical activation just before plating. Removes last passivation layer and ensures maximum adhesion.
Chemical
Dilute acid (same chemistry as plating bath) or specific activator solution
Time
30 sec to 2 min. Do NOT rinse after — parts go directly into plating bath.
NO RINSE AFTER
LAST STEP BEFORE PLATE
🫧
STEP PRE-05
Ultrasonic Cleaning (Optional)
Purpose
For complex-shaped parts, blind holes, or tight tolerances. Cavitation bubbles blast contamination from hard-to-reach areas.
Frequency
20–40 kHz, combined with alkaline cleaner solution
When Used
Electronics, hydraulic fittings, precision parts with complex geometry
PRECISION PARTS
COMPLEX SHAPES

STEP PL-01
Strike Bath
Purpose
Thin high-current flash deposit to establish adhesion before the main bath. Without it, plating can peel.
Current
High current density (2–3× normal) for short time (30 sec–2 min)
Example
Copper strike before nickel on steel; Wood's nickel strike before chrome on stainless steel
ADHESION LAYER
HIGH CURRENT
🔋
STEP PL-02A
Zinc Plating Bath
Purpose
Most common industrial plating — corrosion protection for steel hardware, fasteners, automotive parts.
Chemistry
Alkaline zinc (ZnO + NaOH) or acid zinc chloride bath
Parameters
Zn²⁺: 8–15 g/L | Current: 1–4 A/dm² | Temp: 20–35°C
Thickness
5–25 microns standard; 25+ heavy-duty
MOST COMMON
BARREL / RACK

STEP PL-02B
Nickel Plating Bath
Purpose
Decorative finish + corrosion barrier. Often used as undercoat before chrome plating.
Chemistry
Watts bath: NiSO₄ + NiCl₂ + H₃BO₃, pH 3.5–4.5, 45–60°C
Thickness
5–50 µm decorative; up to 500 µm engineering nickel

STEP PL-02C
Hard Chrome / Decorative Chrome
Purpose
Hard chrome: extreme wear resistance for hydraulics, molds. Decorative chrome: bright shiny surface over nickel.
Chemistry
CrO₃ + H₂SO₄ catalyst, 45–55°C. Cr6⁺ — toxic, heavily regulated.
Hard Chrome
25–500+ µm | 65–70 HRC hardness | Excellent wear + release
Dec. Chrome
0.1–0.5 µm only — purely for appearance over nickel base

STEP PL-02D
Electroless Nickel Plating
Purpose
No electricity needed — chemical auto-catalytic reaction deposits perfectly uniform nickel inside holes and cavities.
Chemistry
NiSO₄ + sodium hypophosphite (reducing agent), 85–92°C, pH 4.5–5.5
Key Benefit
100% uniform thickness everywhere — critical for precision parts, valves, molds
Hardness
As-plated 48–52 HRC; heat treated up to 68 HRC
UNIFORM COVERAGE
NO ELECTRICITY
HIGH TEMP
🌡️
STEP PL-02E
Anodizing (Aluminum)
Purpose
Grows a thick aluminum oxide layer ON the part surface (not deposited). Makes aluminum harder, corrosion-resistant, and dyeable.
Chemistry
H₂SO₄ 15–20%, 18–22°C, 12–18V DC. Hard anodize: 0°C, 50–100V.
Process
Anodize → Dye (optional color) → Seal (boiling DI water or nickel acetate)
Thickness
5–25 µm decorative; 25–100+ µm hard coat
ALUMINUM ONLY
COLORABLE
GROWS INWARD

STEP PL-02F
Phosphating (Zinc / Manganese / Iron)
Purpose
Creates a crystalline phosphate conversion coating on steel. Used as paint primer or wear/corrosion layer.
Zinc Phos.
Heavy automotive prep for paint; thick crystal layer, grey color
Manganese
Dark grey; used on gears, engine parts for break-in wear resistance
Iron Phos.
Light coating, mainly for paint adhesion on sheet metal
PAINT PREP
WEAR RESIST
NO ELECTRICITY

STEP PL-03
Drag-Out Recovery Tank
Purpose
Parts lifted from bath carry plating solution ("drag-out"). This still-rinse tank recovers that chemical before waste water.
Function
Concentrated drag-out is pumped BACK into the main bath — saves chemicals and reduces waste load
Benefit
Recovers 70–90% of drag-out value; critical for expensive metals (silver, gold, tin)
COST SAVING

Zone 03 — Post-Treatment & Finishing

After plating, parts need sealing, passivation, and drying to lock in corrosion protection. High-strength steel parts also need baking to eliminate hydrogen embrittlement risk.

🛡️
STEP POST-01
Chromate Conversion / Passivation
Purpose
Applied over zinc or cadmium plating to dramatically increase corrosion resistance. The chromate film self-heals microscopic scratches.
Types
Clear/Blue (least) → Yellow/Iridescent → Olive/Thick (maximum protection)
Chemistry
Trivalent Cr3⁺ (RoHS-compliant, modern) or hexavalent Cr6⁺ (older, restricted)
Salt Spray
Zinc alone: 24–72 hrs | + Clear passivate: 120–200 hrs | + Thick passivate: 400–500 hrs
SELF-HEALING
Cr3⁺ PREFERRED
Cr6⁺ RESTRICTED
💦
STEP POST-02
Final Rinse (DI Water)
Purpose
Removes all chemical residue. DI water leaves no mineral stains or spots on the finished surface.
Water Quality
Conductivity <50 µS/cm. Tap water leaves calcium/mineral stains on bright surfaces.
Cascade
2–3 counter-flow tanks. Last tank always uses fresh DI. Spent water routed to waste treatment.
DI WATER
SPOT-FREE
🔥
STEP POST-03
Drying Oven
Purpose
Removes all moisture to prevent white rust (zinc) or staining. Also cures passivate films and sealer coatings.
Temp
60–80°C for zinc/chromate. Do NOT exceed 70°C for Cr3⁺ passivate — cracks the film.
Method
Centrifuge spin-dryer for barrel parts; conveyor oven for rack parts
Time
10–20 minutes at temperature
TEMP CRITICAL
CONVEYOR / BATCH
⚛️
STEP POST-04
De-Hydrogenation (Baking)
Purpose
High-strength steel absorbs hydrogen during acid pickling and plating. This causes embrittlement — cracks under load. Baking drives the hydrogen out.
Parameters
190–210°C for minimum 8 hours, within 4 hours of plating (ASTM B633 / ISO 9587)
Who Needs It
High-tensile bolts, springs, aerospace fasteners — any hardened steel >39 HRC
SAFETY CRITICAL
ASTM B633
HIGH STRENGTH ONLY
🧴
STEP POST-05
Topcoat / Sealer (Optional)
Purpose
Organic sealer or wax applied over passivate to further increase corrosion performance and improve appearance.
Types
Silicate sealer, water-based lacquer, wax topcoat, or lubricant coating for fasteners
Benefit
Adds 200–400 hrs extra salt spray protection. Adds lubricity to threaded fasteners.
EXTRA PROTECTION
DIPPED OR SPRAYED
🔩
STEP POST-06
Unracking / Demasking
Purpose
Parts removed from racks, masked areas uncovered, parts separated from tooling fixtures.
Steps
Remove rack tape/plugs → Visual check for rack marks → Rework decision → Send to QC
Rework
Parts with adhesion failure can be stripped and re-processed from the beginning
MANUAL STEP
QC HANDOFF

Zone 04 — Machines & Tank Equipment

Every tank requires supporting equipment to maintain correct process conditions — heaters, blowers, PLC automation, and fume extraction are the mechanical backbone of the factory.

🏗️
EQUIPMENT 01
Tank Design & Layout
Materials
PP for acid tanks | PVC for mild alkaline | Steel + rubber lining for hot alkaline | Steel for chrome
Dimensions
Sized to fit largest workpiece with 150mm clearance all sides. Typically 500–3000L volume.
Layout
Linear (in-line) or U-shape. Tanks numbered and color-coded. Compatible chemicals grouped together.
Spill
Secondary containment bund at 110% capacity around each tank to catch leaks.
CHEMICAL COMPATIBLE
SECONDARY CONTAIN
🌡️
EQUIPMENT 02
Heaters
Immersion
Titanium or PTFE-coated elements. Used in alkaline, neutral, and some acid baths.
Quartz
Used in HF or highly oxidizing solutions where titanium would corrode. Fragile — handle carefully.
Steam
External steam coil or heat exchanger for large-volume hot tanks (cleaner, phosphate)
Control
Thermostat + PLC controls temperature to ±2°C. Over-temp alarm prevents bath degradation.
Ti or PTFE LINED
±2°C CONTROL
💨
EQUIPMENT 03
Blowers & Agitation
Purpose
Agitation replenishes metal ions at the part surface, removes trapped hydrogen gas, and ensures even coating distribution.
Air Agitation
Low-pressure air through PVC perforated pipe at tank bottom. Simple and effective.
Cathode Rod
Mechanical rack oscillation — most uniform plating distribution method
Pump Circ.
Pump + filter unit circulates solution — dual purpose: agitation + filtration
UNIFORM DEPOSIT
PUMP+FILTER COMBO

Cranes & Hoists
Manual

Automatic
PLC-controlled auto hoist — programmed sequence, timed immersion. Common in high-volume lines.
Capacity
50–500 kg per lift. SWL (Safe Working Load) rated and certified.
Material
FRP (fiberglass) or PVC-coated steel to resist chemical splash

Zone 06 — Environmental Safety & Waste Treatment

Surface treatment factories generate hazardous liquid waste. Chinese regulations (GB 21900-2008) set strict discharge limits. Proper waste water treatment is both a legal requirement and an operational necessity.

⚗️
WASTE 01
Chromium Reduction (Cr6⁺ → Cr3⁺)
Why Critical
Hexavalent Cr6⁺ is a known carcinogen — most hazardous waste stream in a plating factory. Must be destroyed before discharge.
Process
Lower pH to 2–3 with H₂SO₄ → Add sodium metabisulfite → Cr6⁺ reduced to Cr3⁺ → Raise pH to 8–9 → Cr3⁺ precipitates as Cr(OH)₃ sludge
Discharge
Cr3⁺ <0.5 mg/L | Cr6⁺ <0.1 mg/L (China GB 21900)
MOST HAZARDOUS
REDUCE FIRST
SLUDGE = HAZWASTE
☣️
WASTE 02
Cyanide Destruction
Source
Silver plating, some gold plating, and older copper strike baths use cyanide chemistry. Extremely toxic.
Process
Alkaline chlorination: pH >11 → sodium hypochlorite → CN⁻ oxidized to CNO⁻ → more hypochlorite → CNO⁻ → N₂ + CO₂
Warning
CN⁻ <0.5 mg/L discharge limit. NEVER mix cyanide with acid — produces HCN gas (lethal).
LETHAL — SEGREGATE
ALKALINE DESTROY
⚖️
WASTE 03
pH Adjustment & Metal Precipitation
Purpose
All metal ions (Ni²⁺, Zn²⁺, Cu²⁺) must be removed from rinse water by raising pH until they precipitate as metal hydroxides.
Process
Neutralize to pH 8.5–10 using NaOH or lime milk → Metal ions precipitate → Add flocculant (PAM) → Settle in clarifier
Discharge pH
China GB 21900: pH 6–9 | Ni <0.1, Zn <1.5, Cu <0.3 mg/L
pH 6–9 TARGET
FLOCCULANT ADDED
🏔️
WASTE 04
Sludge & Solid Waste Handling
What is it
Settled metal hydroxide precipitate containing concentrated heavy metals — classified as hazardous waste.
Processing
Filter press dewaters sludge → 30–50% solid "filter cake" → Sealed drums → Licensed hazardous waste contractor disposes
Records
Full chain of custody required by Chinese law. Cannot be buried or illegally dumped.
HAZARDOUS WASTE
LICENSED DISPOSAL
RECORDS REQUIRED
